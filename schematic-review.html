Schematic Review Service

Expert evaluation of your circuit design for electrical correctness, robustness, protection strategy, and best-practice compliance - before a single trace is routed.

What ERC misses - and why it matters

ERC finds wiring errors. It does not check whether your power sequencing is correct, whether your decoupling values are appropriate, whether your reset circuit works reliably across temperature, or whether your ESD protection is rated correctly. That requires engineering judgment.

Power supply design and sequencing

We verify that power rails are designed correctly for their load, that sequencing follows IC requirements, and that startup and shutdown behaviour is safe across all conditions. Incorrect sequencing is a leading cause of IC latch-up in production.

Decoupling strategy

We review decoupling capacitor selection across the full schematic: bulk, mid-frequency, and high-frequency decoupling per power domain, value selection against IC requirements, and appropriate use of ferrite beads for noise isolation between domains.

Reset circuitry and startup

Reset circuits must function correctly across voltage ramp rates, temperature extremes, and brownout conditions. We check that reset IC specs match the MCU's requirements, that RC networks are correctly calculated, and that watchdog requirements are addressed.

Protection circuits

We verify ESD protection on all externally exposed IO, reverse-polarity protection appropriate to the supply topology, overcurrent protection where required, and that TVS and transient protection devices have appropriate clamping voltages and response times for the application.

Communication interfaces

Each interface - I²C, SPI, UART, USB, CAN, Ethernet - is reviewed for correct signal levels, termination, and connector pinout compliance. I²C pull-up values are calculated against bus capacitance for the correct rise time. USB D+/D− compliance, CAN termination, and RS485 fail-safe are all verified.

Crystal and clock networks

Crystal oscillator circuits are reviewed for correct load capacitor values, appropriate drive current settings, and layout recommendations for routing. PLL configuration and reference clock accuracy are reviewed where applicable.

Footprints, symbols, and pinout validation

For microcontrollers, FPGAs, and all other ICs, we cross-reference schematic symbols against component datasheets - pin count, pin assignment, polarity, and special-function pin handling. Footprint mismatches between schematic and reality are a significant source of first-spin failures.
