About

Senior engineers. Practical feedback.

Our reviews are grounded in real-world product development - across consumer electronics, medical devices, and industrial systems. We've felt the pain of finding a critical error after fabrication. That's why we do this.

Hardware engineering with real product experience

pcbreview.com was founded by a group of senior hardware engineers with a common frustration: too many teams were going to fabrication with preventable errors, because they had no access to a senior engineer who could review the design before it went to board.

We've worked in environments where a design error meant a missed product launch. We've sat in pre-compliance test chambers watching a product fail with a week before the regulatory deadline. We've received production boards and found a footprint error that made every unit on the line non-functional.

That experience - specifically the painful parts - is what makes our reviews useful. We review designs the way we'd want our own reviewed: specific, prioritised by real-world impact, and written for engineers who need to act on the findings.

What makes our reviews different

We don't run automated checks and format the output as a report. We go through your design manually - schematic, layout, BOM - and apply engineering judgment to your specific circuit, in the context of what it's trying to do. Every finding is referenced to something concrete. Every recommendation is actionable.

Our clients tell us our reports read like advice from a senior colleague. That's what we're trying to produce.

How we work

Our approach to every review

🎯

Practical, not academic

We prioritise findings by real-world impact. Every issue in our report is something that matters - a genuine risk, or an opportunity to improve reliability or cost. We don't pad reports with low-priority style observations.

📍

Specific, not vague

Every finding references a specific component, net, or board area. Every recommendation is concrete: what to change and why. We write reports we would want to receive ourselves.

🔒

Confidential by default

All designs are treated as confidential. NDAs are available on request - just mention it when you reach out and we will arrange it before file transfer.
